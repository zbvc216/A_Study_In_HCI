Competition

Early 2017 I was contacted by my former tutor and college from Central Bedfordshire College, he offered me a once in a lifetime opportunity to travel to China... free of cost.

He informed me that there was a competition that the college had been invited to for 3 students to attend with him competing against 4 international teams and 90+ Chinese university teams. I gladly accepted, the biggest down side was that I would have to have jabs in order to travel (my kryptonite!!!).

We landed before the other international teams arrived greeted as almost celebrities in this area of China rarely visited by non nationals. Myself and the team had never been to an area sso untouched by the Western world, this was a place definitely not for tourists. Seeing the real China was aided by 2 Chinese students we had been assigned, Eric and Amanda (their english names) made the week a great one to be forever remembered.

We did not do so well in the competiton, likewise our German counterparts also struggled due to the language barriers. However we were awarded a certificate of excelence and continued to be treated as stars.

Automotives

Throughout my fathers life he was always interested in cars, often having a hobby of selling second hand cars until he retired and took it on as a business. Naturally I began to be interested in cars, mostly the ones I would see on Top Gear or in magazines and movies.

On my 16th birthday I was suprised to hear we would be leaving early in the morning to go to Geneva, initially my mind went to skiing, however there was not a huge suitcase waiting and ready. On the way to the airport i was guessing our destinations true reason, eventually I realised that there would be a huge car show taking place in the Geneva convention center that was attached to the airport.

After this first visit we returned a few years later as the cars we had seen the first year were likely to not be phased out for another few years. Similarly we attended the Goodwood Festival of Speed in Sussex a couple of years later and a few times subsequently. Other events we have attended include: british touring car championships at Silverstone, track days, the World Rally Championship in Wales and various single day events such as off road experiences.

Gaming

I began gaming early on in life with my cousins and their hand-me-down Nintendo games console. Soon enough I had my own console an original PlayStation which I had limited games for but would save up pocket money to buy second hand.

One of my best Christmas gifts I remember was a Gameboy Colour and Pokemon Blue, I played this game as best an 8 year old of the time could. Then came the followup games of which I got Gold, this was when I began to truely be a gamer. I quickly became the person others would come to for help (having gotten a copy earlier than the UK release I had longer to learn).

Fast forward a few years and I got my first laptop, which I primarly used for playing World of Warcraft. All the time I continued to play console for the majority, computer science was still very foreign to me and the thought of buying a gaming PC was unheard of with my friend groups and family.

One day my old laptop just wouldnt cut it and as I now had a source of income I looked into getting a PC for gaming and studies, luckily I found a great deal and am still using I (although components have been updated) to this very day and continue to game in varied ways.
